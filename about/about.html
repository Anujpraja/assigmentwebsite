Eden Gardens is a cricket ground in Kolkata, India. Established in 1864, it is the oldest[3][4][5] and second-largest cricket stadium in India and third-largest in the world. The stadium currently has a capacity of 68,000.[3] On 22 November 2019, the venue hosted the first ever day/night Test match in India during the second Test between India and Bangladesh.

Wankhede Stadium

The Wankhede Stadium, Pronunciation is an international cricket stadium in Mumbai. The stadium now has a capacity of 33,108, following renovations for the 2011 Cricket World Cup. Before the upgrade, the capacity was approximately 45,000.

The stadium has been host to numerous high-profile cricket matches in the past, most notably the 2011 Cricket World Cup Final, in which India defeated Sri Lanka and became the first country to win the cricket world cup on home soil. The stadium played host to the last match of Sachin Tendulkar's international career.

Dharamshala, Kangra, Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium,abbreviated as the HPCA Stadium, is a picturesque cricket stadium located in the city of Dharamshala, Kangra district, Himachal Pradesh, India. The town of Dharamsala is best known internationally as the home of the Dalai Lama of Tibet. It is also considered as one of the most beautiful cricket grounds in the world.
